Getting Started

What is Lalabuba?

Lalabuba is a free, AI-powered coloring page generator designed for children. You type or say any subject — a dragon, a bunny, a spaceship — and the AI instantly creates a unique color-by-number or freehand coloring page for your child to color. No templates, no recycled clipart: every picture is freshly generated just for you.

Is Lalabuba completely free? Are there hidden costs, subscriptions, or in-app purchases?

Yes, Lalabuba is 100% free. There are no subscriptions, no in-app purchases, no ads, and no premium tiers. Every feature — AI image generation, all difficulty levels, saving, printing, community gallery, family groups — is included at no cost. We are committed to keeping Lalabuba free for every child on the planet.

Do I need to create an account or sign in to use Lalabuba?

No account is required to draw and color. You can start generating coloring pages immediately without signing up. An optional community profile lets you join the leaderboard and share artwork to the community gallery, but this requires a parent or guardian's approval first.

What ages is Lalabuba designed for?

Lalabuba is designed for children of all ages. The Easy difficulty with large, simple regions is perfect for toddlers aged 2–4. Medium and Hard levels suit children aged 5–10, while Extreme difficulty challenges older kids and even adults. Voice input (just say the subject out loud) makes Lalabuba accessible to pre-readers.

Which devices and browsers does Lalabuba work on?

Lalabuba works in any modern browser on any device: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge on desktop, tablet, and smartphone. Native apps are available for iOS (iPhone and iPad via the App Store) and Android (via Google Play), both free to download.

Is there a native mobile app, and where can I download it?

Yes. The Lalabuba app is available on the Apple App Store for iOS/iPadOS and on Google Play for Android. Search for "Lalabuba" or visit lalabuba.com from your phone — you'll see a download link. The apps are free and include all features.

How do I make my very first coloring page?
  1. Open lalabuba.com or launch the Lalabuba app.
  2. Type what you want to color in the text field — for example "unicorn in a rainbow garden".
  3. Optionally pick a difficulty (Easy, Medium, Hard, or Extreme) and a color count.
  4. Tap the Draw! button.

Within seconds, your unique coloring page appears, ready to color!

What can I type as a subject? Are there any limits?

You can type almost anything child-friendly: animals, characters, scenes, vehicles, food, fantasy creatures, and more. The AI works best with clear, descriptive subjects like "kitten playing with yarn" rather than very abstract ideas. Subjects must be appropriate for children — the AI automatically refuses inappropriate requests. Maximum subject length is 80 characters.

What does the 💡 Surprise me button do?

The 💡 Surprise me button picks a random fun subject for you and fills it into the prompt field. It's great for moments when you're not sure what to draw, or when kids want something unexpected. Each tap gives a completely different idea chosen from a curated list of child-friendly subjects.

What are the suggestion cards on the home screen, and how does the Shuffle button work?

The four colorful cards on the home screen are pre-made subject suggestions. Tap any card to instantly start drawing that subject. The 🎲 Shuffle button replaces all four cards with a fresh set of suggestions — perfect for browsing ideas until something catches your child's eye.

Drawing & Coloring

How does color-by-number work?

After the AI generates your coloring page, each region is outlined and numbered. Each number corresponds to a color in the palette shown on the side. Tap a numbered region to fill it with the currently selected color. As you fill all regions, a picture gradually comes to life. You can complete as much or as little as you like.

How do I switch to free-form coloring without numbers?

Select 🎨 Free Color (tap the Free Color chip in the settings). In this mode, the numbered regions are hidden and you can tap any area to fill it with any color you choose — no rules, no numbers, pure creative freedom.

What is the difference between Tap mode, Pencil mode, and Brush mode?
  • Tap mode (default) fills a closed region with a single tap — great for quick, neat coloring.
  • Pencil mode lets you draw freehand lines and details on top of the coloring.
  • Brush mode works like pencil but with a softer, wider stroke.

You can switch between modes at any time.

What do the four difficulty levels change?
  • Easy — very large, simple regions; ideal for toddlers.
  • Medium — more detail and smaller regions; good for ages 5+.
  • Hard — complex images with fine detail; for older children.
  • Extreme — highly detailed illustrations that challenge even adults.

Difficulty also affects the number of color regions generated.

How many colors are in the palette, and can I change that number?

You can choose between 6, 8, 12, or 99 colors. The 🎨 chip in the settings bar cycles through these options. Fewer colors keep things simple for younger children; 99 colors ("Max") gives the richest coloring experience. This setting is remembered between sessions.

How do I undo a fill or a pencil stroke?

Tap the ↩ Undo button in the canvas toolbar. Each tap undoes one action — fills, pencil strokes, and color selections. There is no limit on the number of undos within a session.

How do I zoom in and out on my drawing?

Use a standard pinch-to-zoom gesture on touch screens to zoom in and out. On desktop, use your mouse scroll wheel or trackpad pinch. Double-tap resets the zoom to fit the screen. Zooming makes it much easier to color small, detailed regions precisely.

Can I pan the image when zoomed in?

Yes. When zoomed in, drag with one finger (or click and drag on desktop) to pan the canvas. On touch screens, use two fingers to pan while pinching to zoom. Lalabuba automatically distinguishes between a pan gesture and a coloring tap.

What does the Again! button do — does it use the same subject?

The 🎲 Again! button generates a brand-new coloring page using the same subject you typed. Because the AI creates a unique image every time, you get a completely different picture of the same theme — useful when you want variety without retyping.

How do I draw freehand on top of my coloring page?

Tap the ✏️ Draw button in the canvas toolbar to enter freehand drawing mode. You can then draw anywhere on the canvas — add details, write your name, or decorate freely. On Easy and Medium difficulty, freehand strokes automatically stay within the lines to help younger children.

How do I clear my freehand drawing without erasing my coloring?

In pencil mode, a dedicated clear-pencil button removes only your freehand strokes while keeping all color fills intact. The regular Undo button also works stroke by stroke in pencil mode.

What does "I'm finished!" do, and when does it appear?

The "I'm finished!" button appears when you've colored approximately 45% or more of the page, or when Lalabuba detects you're done. Tapping it celebrates your work with a reward animation, saves your progress, and unlocks the sharing and printing options. You can tap it any time to finish whenever you feel ready.

How long does it typically take to generate a coloring page?

Most coloring pages appear in 5–15 seconds. Generation time depends on server load and your internet connection. A bouncing animation plays while you wait. If a page takes longer than usual, it will still arrive — please be patient and avoid refreshing the page.

Why does the page sometimes take longer to appear?

Generation time increases during peak hours when many children around the world are using Lalabuba simultaneously. Complex subjects and Extreme difficulty also take slightly longer. If generation is taking very long, try a simpler subject or a lower difficulty level.

Canvas & Palettes

Can I change the background color of the canvas?

Yes. In the canvas settings you can choose from several background colors including white, light yellow (warm paper), and transparent. The background color affects how the final saved image looks.

Can I change the shape of the coloring canvas?

Yes. Lalabuba supports Square (default), Circle, Oval, and Diamond canvas shapes. Square and Circle are free; Oval and Diamond are unlocked by coloring a certain number of pages. The shape affects how the image is cropped and how it looks when saved or printed.

How do I add a decorative frame around my coloring page?

In the canvas settings, look for the Frame option. Classic and No-frame options are free. Additional decorative frames are unlocked as rewards for coloring more pages. Frames appear in the saved and printed version of your artwork.

What color palettes are available?

The free palettes include Classic (vibrant primary colors), Pastel (soft, gentle tones), and Nature (earthy greens and browns). Additional crayon packs — Neon, Candy, and Galaxy — are unlocked as rewards for completing coloring pages.

What are the Neon, Candy, and Galaxy crayon packs, and how do I unlock them?
  • Neon — bright, glowing colors.
  • Candy — sweet pastel and bright sweet-shop tones.
  • Galaxy — deep cosmic blues, purples, and silver.

Each is unlocked by completing a certain number of coloring pages in total — the reward tracker in your Journal shows your progress toward each unlock.

How many pictures do I need to color to unlock each crayon pack?

The exact thresholds are shown in the Stickers & Rewards section of your Journal. Generally, Neon unlocks earliest (after a few completed pages), followed by Candy, then Galaxy. Completing pages at any difficulty level counts toward unlocks.

Printing & Saving

How do I print my coloring page?

Tap the 🖨️ Print button in the canvas toolbar. You can choose to print the blank line art (for coloring on paper by hand), your finished colored version, or the color-by-number template with the numbered guide. The print dialog opens your browser's standard print panel.

Can I print the blank line art to color on paper by hand?

Yes. When you tap Print, one of the options is "Line art only" — this prints the clean black-and-white outlines with no fills and no numbers, perfect for coloring with real crayons, markers, or watercolors on paper.

How do I save or download my coloring page as an image file?

Tap the 💾 Save button in the canvas toolbar. This downloads your current coloring state as a PNG image file to your device. On mobile, the image is saved to your photo library or Downloads folder. On desktop, it downloads to your default Downloads folder.

Daily Challenge & Explore

What is the Daily Challenge (Today's Word)?

Every day, Lalabuba reveals a special "Today's Word" — a themed subject that all users around the world can color together. Tap the daily word pill to instantly use it as your subject. Completing the daily challenge earns bonus points toward your leaderboard score and can reward special stickers.

Does everyone get the same daily word on the same day?

Yes. The daily word is the same for all Lalabuba users worldwide. This means children across different countries are all coloring the same theme on the same day — a fun shared experience. The word resets at midnight UTC each day.

What is the "Today's coloring page" feature?

Today's coloring page is a freshly generated coloring page based on the daily word, updated every day. It's a quick way to start coloring without typing anything — just open the page and start!

How do I browse coloring pages by topic?

Visit lalabuba.com/coloring-pages/ to find topic hubs for popular subjects like dragons, unicorns, dinosaurs, cats, butterflies, mermaids, princesses, and rockets. Each hub lets you generate new ones instantly.

Community Gallery & Sharing

How do I share my artwork with the Lalabuba community gallery?

After finishing a coloring page, tap the 🌟 Community button. If you haven't set up a community profile yet, you'll be asked to choose a nickname and emoji avatar, and a parent or guardian must approve community sharing. Then choose what to share and confirm. Your artwork appears in the community gallery within seconds.

What is the difference between sharing my coloring, the template, and my freehand drawing?
  • Coloring — posts your finished colored image to the gallery.
  • Template — posts the blank line-art so others can color the same image themselves.
  • Freehand — posts your freestyle drawing on top of the coloring.
Why does sharing artwork require parental consent?

To comply with international child-safety laws (COPPA in the US, GDPR Article 8 in Europe), any feature that posts content publicly requires a parent or guardian to explicitly approve it first. The consent is given once and remembered for future shares.

What is a community nickname, and how do I choose one?

A community nickname is a fun, child-safe display name used in the gallery and on the leaderboard. Lalabuba uses a curated system of adjective + animal combos (like "Sparkly Dragon" or "Bouncy Penguin") that are always appropriate and never reveal personal information. You pick from a list or spin to get a random one.

Can I change my community nickname or avatar later?

Yes. You can change your nickname and emoji avatar in Settings > Community Profile. There is a minimum interval between nickname changes (typically 7 days) to prevent confusion in the community. Avatar changes can be made anytime.

How do I star or like someone else's artwork?

In the community gallery, tap any artwork to open it full-size, then tap the ⭐ Star button. Your star is added to that artwork's total count. You can star as many artworks as you like, and remove your star by tapping again. Stars contribute to the leaderboard weekly score.

How do I report an inappropriate artwork?

In the full-size view of any artwork, tap the 🚩 report button. After 3 reports from different users, an artwork is automatically hidden pending review. Please report anything that seems inappropriate — community safety is a top priority.

What is the "Color this too!" button on a community artwork?

When a child shared their coloring as a template, a "Color this too!" button appears in the full-size gallery view. Tapping it loads that same line-art for you to color in your own way — a fun creative challenge to see how differently two children can color the same picture.

What is the Leaderboard, and how is it scored?

The Leaderboard shows the top community members ranked by their weekly or all-time score. Your score is based on the total number of coloring pages completed and the stars your shared artworks have received. You don't need to share artwork to appear — completing pages earns you a score.

Is there a weekly leaderboard and an all-time leaderboard?

Yes. The weekly leaderboard resets every Monday and celebrates the most active colorers of that week. The all-time leaderboard tracks total pages completed since you first started. Your own position is highlighted so you can always see where you rank.

Challenges

How do I challenge a friend to color the same picture?

After generating a coloring page, tap the 🏆 Challenge button. This creates a unique challenge link and QR code for your current image. Share the link or QR code with a friend — when they open it, they get the same image to color.

How does the QR code challenge work?

The QR code encodes a link to your specific coloring page. Anyone who scans it (or taps the link) instantly opens that same coloring page in Lalabuba — on their phone, tablet, or computer. It's a fun way to start a coloring race at home or in a classroom.

Who wins a coloring challenge?

Lalabuba challenges are friendly and non-competitive — there's no winner or loser. Both colorers complete the same picture in their own style and can share their results. The goal is creative fun and comparison, not competition. Great for siblings or classmates!

Family Groups

What is a Family Group?

A Family Group connects up to 6 family members so they can see each other's coloring progress, artworks, and streaks. It's completely private — only people with your family code can join. Perfect for siblings, parents and children, or grandparents who want to share the coloring experience.

How do I create a Family Group and get a family code?

In Settings > Family Group, tap "Create Family Code". A parent or guardian must approve this step. You'll receive a unique 6-character code (like "SUNBR7") to share privately with family members.

How does another family member join using the family code?

The other person opens Lalabuba, goes to Settings > Family Group, taps "Join with Code", and enters the 6-character family code. A parent or guardian on their device must approve joining. Once joined, they appear in your family member list.

How many members can a Family Group have?

A Family Group can have up to 6 members. If you need to add a new member and are at the limit, an existing member would need to leave first.

Can I see my family members' colored pictures and their progress?

Yes. In the Family Group view, you can see each member's nickname, emoji avatar, total pages completed, current streak, and their 3 most recent artworks as thumbnails. This makes it fun to cheer each other on and see what everyone has been coloring.

How do I leave a Family Group?

In Settings > Family Group, tap "Leave Family" and confirm. You'll leave the group immediately and your activity will no longer be visible to other members. You can join a different family group at any time.

Journal, Stickers & Rewards

What is My Journal?

My Journal is your personal creative diary in the Lalabuba app. It shows all the coloring pages you've completed, organized by date. You can revisit any past coloring, see your stickers and badges, track your streaks, and see your overall coloring statistics — your personal art portfolio.

How do stickers (badges) work, and how do I earn them?

Stickers are achievement badges you earn by reaching milestones. They appear in your Journal and on your community profile. Categories include: Milestones (total pages colored), Day Streaks (consecutive coloring days), Explorer (trying new subjects), Creativity (using different tools and modes), and Sharing & Saving.

What is a day streak, and how do I keep it going?

A day streak counts how many consecutive days you've completed at least one coloring page. Coloring even one page per day keeps your streak alive. If you miss a day, the streak resets to 0. Long streaks earn special streak stickers — visible in your Journal.

What are Daily Missions, and do they change every day?

Daily Missions are small creative challenges that refresh every day — for example, "Color a page on Extreme difficulty" or "Use the Nature palette". Completing missions earns bonus points and contributes to streak stickers. They encourage kids to try different features each day.

What is the mascot penguin, and how do I decorate it?

Lala the penguin mascot lives in your Journal and can be customized with decorative accessories — hats, items, and background scenes. Accessories are unlocked by earning certain stickers or completing milestones. Tap the penguin to open the mascot studio.

What are Scenes, and how do I unlock them?

Scenes are decorative backgrounds for your mascot: Meadow, Iceberg, Ocean, City, and Space. Each creates a different setting for Lala the penguin. Scenes are unlocked by completing coloring pages and reaching milestones. The Meadow scene is free from the start.

What is the Print Book feature?

The Print Book feature lets you compile multiple coloring pages from your Journal into a single printable PDF booklet. You choose which pages to include, and Lalabuba lays them out in book format with a cover. Print it at home for a personalized coloring book your child made themselves.

Settings & Options

How do I change the app language? Which languages are supported?

Lalabuba supports 12 languages: English, German, French, Spanish, Portuguese, Italian, Dutch, Polish, Russian, Turkish, Chinese (Simplified), and Hindi. On the web, click the language icon in the header or settings panel. In the app, go to Settings > Language. The entire interface switches immediately.

How do I switch between dark mode and light mode?

Tap the 🌙 (moon) or ☀️ (sun) button in the top-right corner of any page to toggle themes. Lalabuba also automatically follows your device's system theme preference when you first open it. Your preference is saved across visits.

How do I turn sound effects on or off?

Sound effects can be toggled in the Settings panel (⚙️ gear icon). This controls in-app sounds such as fill chimes, button taps, and celebration effects. Your preference is saved between sessions.

What is the Read Aloud / narration setting?

When narration is enabled, Lalabuba reads aloud hints, tips, and encouragements as your child colors. This makes the app fully accessible to pre-readers. Narration uses the device's built-in text-to-speech in the selected app language.

How do I use voice input to say my subject out loud?

Tap the 🎤 microphone button inside the prompt field. Speak your subject clearly — for example, say "a puppy in a flower garden". Lalabuba transcribes your words and fills the prompt field automatically. Voice input works in all 12 supported languages.

What is the Classic drawing mode chip?

The 🖊️ Classic chip toggles between "Classic" (traditional thick, clean line-art style) and "Sketch" (a softer, hand-drawn illustration style). Classic is more structured for color-by-number; Sketch gives a more artistic, painterly look. Try both to see which your child prefers.

Account, Privacy & Safety

What happens to my progress if I don't have an account?

Your progress (completed pages, stickers, streaks, crayon unlocks) is stored locally on your device. If you clear your browser data or use a different device, that progress will not carry over. To sync progress across devices, set up a community profile — your stats are then saved to the server.

Does signing in sync my progress across multiple devices?

Yes. When you have a community profile set up, your total completed pages, streaks, and unlocks are synced to our servers each time you complete a page. Your leaderboard rank reflects your actual total, and your progress is not lost if you switch devices.

What information does Lalabuba collect about my child?

Lalabuba collects no personal information unless you opt into community features. No name, email, age, or location is required. Community profiles use only a curated anonymous nickname and an emoji avatar — never a real name or photo. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.

Is Lalabuba safe for young children (COPPA / GDPR child privacy compliance)?

Yes. Lalabuba is designed from the ground up with child safety in mind. We comply with COPPA (US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) and GDPR Article 8 (EU child data protection). No PII is collected from children. Community features require explicit parental consent. All AI-generated content is automatically filtered to be child-appropriate.

Why does the community sharing feature require a parent or guardian to approve?

International child-safety regulations require verifiable parental consent before a child can post content to a public community. The consent is a simple confirmation that a parent or guardian reads and approves — it's stored so you only need to do it once.

How do I manage cookie preferences?

Lalabuba uses only essential technical cookies and localStorage for saving your settings (theme, language, progress). We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking cookies. For any privacy-related requests, contact us at info@lalabuba.com or visit our Privacy Policy.

Troubleshooting

A "tap to confirm you're human" verification appeared — is that normal?

Yes. Lalabuba uses Cloudflare Turnstile (an invisible, privacy-friendly CAPTCHA alternative) to prevent bots from abusing our AI image generation. It usually completes automatically with no action needed. On some networks it may show a small checkbox. This is completely normal and takes only a second.

The coloring page is taking much longer than usual — what should I do?

During peak times, image generation can take up to 60 seconds. Please do not refresh the page — this cancels the current generation. If the page is still loading after 2 minutes, a "Try again" message will appear. Check your internet connection and try a simpler subject or lower difficulty.

Lalabuba isn't working in my browser — what should I try?
  1. Make sure you're using an up-to-date browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge).
  2. Try a hard refresh: Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows, Cmd+Shift+R on Mac.
  3. Temporarily disable browser extensions, especially ad blockers.
  4. If problems persist, contact us at info@lalabuba.com.
Does Lalabuba work offline?

AI image generation requires an internet connection — the AI runs on our servers. However, once a coloring page has loaded, you can continue coloring it offline. Saving and printing also work offline once the page is loaded. Your progress is saved locally and synced when you reconnect.

I'm on mobile and the tap-to-fill isn't registering correctly — what should I do?
  1. Make sure you're tapping directly on a numbered region, not on a black outline (outlines aren't fillable areas).
  2. Try zooming in for more precise tapping on small regions.
  3. In Pencil mode, make sure you're drawing with a single finger (not your palm).
  4. If taps still miss, try the Lalabuba app instead of the mobile browser for the best touch responsiveness.
